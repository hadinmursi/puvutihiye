Highlights

Long known for not just his acting prowess on Friends but also his willingness to share both his personal struggles and triumphs with the world, Matthew Perry was something of an icon.

Not only did Matthew Perry prove he was the perfect Chandler Bing, but he became a 'friend' to everyone who watched him act. Sadly, after seemingly triumphing over his personal struggles, Matthew Perry passed away on October 28th, 2023.

TMZ was one of the first news outlets to report the news, and immediately, fans began flocking to Perry's social media accounts to see if the news was real, pay their respects, and lament his passing. Fans would soon find that Perry's final Instagram post was quite eerie, for a very tragic reason.

Matthew Perry Reportedly Died Of Drowning At His Home

According to early reports, Matthew Perry passed away at his home in the Los Angeles area on the evening of October 28th. His cause of death was said to be due to drowning; first responders were responding to a call for cardiac arrest.

It's stated that Matthew was found in the jacuzzi on his property, but as TMZ noted, the exact location was unknown. Details were limited upon the first report, but TMZ suggested there was no foul play involved and that there were no drugs found on site.

What is known is that the report noted Perry was in his hot tub at the time of his passing, and tragically, his final Instagram post might indicate more about how he passed away.

Matthew Perry's Final Instagram Post Was An Image Of Him In The Jacuzzi

Fans hoping to scroll to Matthew Perry's Instagram and find him there refuting headlines about his death were sorely disappointed and in disbelief. While older posts on Perry's Instagram had a few hundred comments each, his final post from October 23rd had racked up thousands of comments within an hour of the report that he had passed.

Matthew's last Instagram post featured a snapshot of him in a hot tub at nighttime, ostensibly at his Los Angeles-area home. Lights from other homes can be seen in the background, and the moon is peeking out from behind the clouds.

The caption on the image read, "Oh, so warm water swirling around makes you feel good? I'm Mattman."
