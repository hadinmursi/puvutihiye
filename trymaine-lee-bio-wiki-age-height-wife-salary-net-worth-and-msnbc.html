Trymaine Lee Emmy, Documentary

In October 2018, Lee earned his first Emmy Award for his coverage of gun violence in Chicago. He received the honor at the 39th Annual News and Documentary Emmy Awards at Jazz at Lincoln Center’s Frederick P. Rose Hall.

Lee was recognized for his reporting for the documentary “All in America: Chicago.” The hourlong documentary featured a town hall meeting, a panel discussion, and interviews with Black residents in South Chicago.

Trymaine Lee Pulitzer

In 2006 “Nightmare in the 9th Ward all too real for one woman” was one of the ten stories cited when The Times-Picayune staff won the Pulitzer Prize for Breaking News Reporting. Lee shared the award with three other reporters, Doug MacCash, Manuel Torres, and Mark Schleifstein.

The award marked the first time a Pulitzer was awarded for online journalism. Lee also contributed to coverage of the Eliot Spitzer prostitution scandal by The New York Times, which won the Breaking News Pulitzer three years later.
