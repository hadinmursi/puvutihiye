Tristan Thompson Biography

Tristan Thompson is a Canadian-American professional basketball player. He currently plays for NBA’s Cleveland Cavaliers. He had a daughter with reality star Khloe Kardashian in April 2018. A center and power forward, he has played for the Cavaliers since December 2012 and has won one NBA championship with the team.

Tristan Thompson Family and Education

Tristan was born in Brampton, Ontario, Canada. His parents are Trevor and Andrea Thompson. Not much is really known of his family nor his past life at the moment. Coming to his educational background, He worked as a paperboy in his hometown before relocating to Newark, New Jersey to attend high school at Saint Benedict’s Preparatory School in his sophomore year.

While at Findlay Prep, he led the school to their first National Championship and was named a 2010 McDonald’s All-American, as well as a Jordan Brand Classic All-American. In 2009, he signed a letter to play at the University of Texas where he was named the most valuable player in the 2010-2011 season.

Leading the team in rebounds, blocks and ranking second for scoring in his freshman year, he was one of five national finalists for the U.S. Basketball Writers Association’s Wayman Tisdale Award. He was also ranked 24th in the nation in blocks per game.

Tristan Thompson Career

Tristan chose to leave The University of Texas at the end of his freshman year in favor of entering the NBA draft in June 2011. He was selected by the Cleveland Cavaliers in the Fourth round, He made his NBA in December the same year. Finishing his first season on the team, he was named to the 2012 NBA All-Rookie Second Team.

He became a starter on the team in 2015, helping the Cavaliers reach the 2015 NBA Finals, though they lost to the Golden State Warriors. At the end of the 2014-2015 season, he became a restricted free agent but ultimately re-signed with the Cavaliers with a five-year, $82 million contract, after four months of negotiations.

At the end of the 2015-2016 season, Thompson and the Cavaliers won an NBA championship, the team’s first championship win in 52 years. Tristan became the first player in Cavaliers history to play in 400 consecutive regular-season games in December 2016. That season and the following one both ended with losses to the Warriors in the Finals.
