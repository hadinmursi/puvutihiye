Early Life: Upbringing and Education

Austin Reed Dillon is the elder son of former stock car racer for Richard Childress Racing, Mike Dillon and Tina Dillon. Also, he is a grandchild of former NASCAR driver; Richard Childress.

He was born on April 27, 1990, Piedmont – Triad area of North Carolina, United States of America. He completed his schooling at Forsyth Country Day school, and then he attended his university from High Point University.

Initially, Austin thought he would become a baseball player, but later, when he tested the bandolero car for the first time, he was hooked.

Four-wheel racing, cars, and track impacted his childhood as his father and grandfather both were from motorsport backgrounds. In an interview, he stated that racing was always a topic at the dinner table.

Racing Career of the Ace

The Ace played in the little league world series in 2002  in Pennsylvania. Initially, he began in the Bandoleros series, an entry-level car powered by a Stratton engine and single cylinder Briggs.

Afterward, he switched to higher-powered legends cars, which have four-cylinder motorcycle engines of Yamaha.

Again, he moved to the late model series and ran on Dirt oval tracks, which have 20 times more horsepower than the entry-level car Bandolero.

In the year 2008 A.D., he became Rookie of the year in the NASCAR Camping World East Series.

Dillon is also the winner of the 2017 Coca-Cola 600, 2018 Daytona 500, and texas motor speedway 2020.

After completing four racers for Andy Santerre Motorsport, he switched to Richard Childress Racing’s banner, which his grandfather owned.
